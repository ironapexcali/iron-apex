Iron Apex Elite Program

This program is designed for athletes who already possess advanced calisthenics strength and want to push toward elite skills such as straddle planche, front lever pullups and one-arm pullups.

Requirements

20+ pullups
Weighted pullup +25kg
Consistent clean muscle ups
Front lever progression
Advanced tuck planche

Primary Skill Goals

• Straddle / full planche
• Front lever pullups
• One arm pullup progressions
• V-sit
• Weighted muscle ups
• Advanced handstand strength

Weekly Structure

Monday — Heavy Pull Strength
Tuesday — Skill / Planche Training
Wednesday — Mobility / Recovery
Thursday — Push Strength
Friday — Skill + Core
Saturday — Explosive Strength
Sunday — Rest

Warmup (10–12 minutes)

Jump rope — 2 minutes Band shoulder rotations — 3 × 20 Scapula pullups — 3 × 12 Dead hang — 2 × 40 seconds Shoulder mobility drills — 4 minutes

Elite level pulling places enormous stress on the elbows and shoulders. Never rush your warmup.

MONDAY — Heavy Pull Strength

Weighted Pullups 6 sets × 4 reps Rest: 3 minutes
Front Lever Pullups 5 × 3
Archer Pullups 4 × 6 each side
Typewriter Pullups 4 × 5
Front Lever Hold 6 × 12 seconds
Hanging Toes to Bar 5 × 12

Focus on strict pulling mechanics. No swinging.

TUESDAY — Planche Skill Training

Planche Leans 5 × 20 seconds
Advanced Tuck Planche 5 × 12 seconds
Straddle Planche Attempts 6 attempts
Pseudo Planche Pushups 5 × 10
Handstand Practice 15 minutes
L-Sit Hold 5 × 30 seconds

Planche progressions require extreme shoulder lean. Keep elbows locked and shoulders protracted.

WEDNESDAY — Mobility Day

Deep squat hold 3 minutes
Shoulder extension stretch 2 minutes
Hamstring stretch 2 minutes
Bridge hold 3 × 30 seconds
Thoracic spine mobility 3 minutes

THURSDAY — Push Strength

Weighted Dips 5 × 6
Handstand Pushups (freestanding or wall) 5 × 6
90 Degree Hold 5 × 10 seconds
Planche Pushups 5 × 4
Wide Handstand Hold 4 × 20 seconds
Plank 4 × 1 minute

FRIDAY — Skill + Core

Muscle Ups 6 × 3
Front Lever Hold Practice 10 minutes
V-Sit Progression 5 × 15 seconds
Compression Raises 4 × 15
Dragon Flags 5 × 6
Hanging Leg Raises 4 × 15

Compression strength is the key element in achieving a full V-sit.

SATURDAY — Explosive Strength

Explosive Pullups 6 × 4
Weighted Muscle Ups 5 × 3
Front Lever Rows 4 × 6
Assisted One Arm Pullups 5 × 3 each arm
Dead Hang 3 × 60 seconds
Dragon Flags 4 × 8

Long Term Skill Goals

Athletes who remain consistent with this program should gradually develop the strength required for the following elite skills:

Straddle planche
Front lever pullups
One arm pullup
V-sit
Clean muscle ups

Recovery Advice

Elite training requires careful recovery management.

Sleep at least 7–9 hours per night.

Eat sufficient calories and protein.

If elbows or shoulders feel strained, reduce volume temporarily.

Consistency and patience are the keys to mastering elite calisthenics skills.
