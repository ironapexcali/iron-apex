IRON APEX BASELINE

This protocol builds the raw strength required for real calisthenics training. The goal is to develop the minimum pulling, pushing and core strength needed before progressing into muscle-up and advanced skill training.

Program Structure

Program length: 8 weeks Training days: 5 per week Session length: 35-60 minutes

The goal of this phase is to develop your **first real bodyweight strength base**. Most beginners fail because they try to train advanced skills without building the necessary pulling strength first.

Equipment Required

You only need basic equipment.

Pull-up Bar

Used for hangs, pull-up progressions and core work.

Dip Bars

Used for dip strength and support holds.

Resistance Band

Optional assistance for pull-up progressions.

Training Rules

Strict Reps Only

Every repetition must use full range of motion with controlled tempo. No half reps and no swinging.

Stop Before Failure

Leave 1-2 reps in reserve each set to maintain good technique and allow faster recovery.

Focus On Form

Perfect mechanics will build strength faster than chasing high rep numbers with bad technique.

Weekly Training Split

Monday — Pull + Core

Dead Hang — 3 sets max time build grip strength
Negative Pull-ups — 4 x 3-5 5 second descent
Australian Rows — 3 x 10-12 chest to bar
Tuck L-Sit — 4 x 15s core compression

Tuesday — Push Strength

Incline Push-ups — 4 x 10 hollow body position
Negative Dips — 4 x 3-5 slow controlled descent
Scapular Push-ups — 3 x 10 shoulder stability
Plank — 3 x 45s core control

Wednesday — Active Recovery

20 minute walk
Light mobility work

Thursday — Pull Strength

Band Assisted Pull-ups — 4 x 6
Scapular Pull-ups — 4 x 10
Hanging Knee Raises — 3 x 10
Passive Hang — 3 sets max

Friday — Push + Core

Strict Push-ups — 4 sets max
Bench Dips — 3 x 12
L-Sit Attempts — 4 x 15s
Hollow Body Hold — 3 x 30s

Saturday — Full Body

Australian Rows — 4 x 12
Push-ups — 4 x 10
Dip Support Hold — 3 x 30s
Knee Raises — 3 x 10

Sunday — Rest

Full recovery day
