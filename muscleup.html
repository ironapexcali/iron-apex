Iron Apex Muscle-Up Program

This program is designed to help athletes achieve clean muscle ups by developing explosive pulling strength, strong bar dips and proper swing mechanics.

Requirements

Minimum 10 pullups
Minimum 12 dips
Basic core strength

Understanding The Swing

A common mistake when learning muscle ups is relying only on pulling strength. In reality, understanding the swing and bar momentum helps position your body for a powerful transition over the bar.

The goal is not to excessively kip, but to use a controlled swing to create efficient upward momentum.

Practice controlled bar swings by maintaining a tight hollow body position and generating power from the shoulders.

Weekly Training Structure

Monday — Pull Strength
Tuesday — Swing + Skill
Wednesday — Mobility
Thursday — Explosive Pull Training
Friday — Transition Training
Saturday — Strength + Volume
Sunday — Rest

Warmup

Jump rope — 2 minutes Band shoulder rotations — 3×20 Scapula pullups — 3×10 Dead hang — 2×30 seconds Light pullups — 2×5

Proper warmups protect the shoulders during explosive pulling.

MONDAY — Pull Strength

Weighted Pullups 5 × 5
Pullups 4 × 10
High Pullups (chest to bar) 5 × 4
Front Lever Tuck Hold 5 × 12 seconds
Hanging Leg Raises 4 × 12

High pullups are one of the most important exercises for muscle ups.

TUESDAY — Swing + Skill

Bar Swing Practice 10 minutes
Band Assisted Muscle Ups 8 attempts
Jumping Muscle Ups 6 attempts
Straight Bar Dips 5 × 8
L-Sit Hold 4 × 20 seconds

WEDNESDAY — Mobility

Deep squat hold — 3 minutes
Shoulder stretch — 2 minutes
Thoracic mobility drills — 3 minutes
Light core work — 5 minutes

THURSDAY — Explosive Pull Training

Explosive Pullups 6 × 4
Chest to Bar Pullups 5 × 5
High Pullups (belly button height) 4 × 4
Front Lever Rows 4 × 6
Toes to Bar 4 × 10

The higher you pull, the easier the muscle up transition becomes.

FRIDAY — Transition Training

Muscle Up Attempts 8 attempts
Low Bar Muscle Ups 5 × 3
Straight Bar Dips 4 × 10
Negative Muscle Ups 4 × 3
Hanging Knee Raises 4 × 15

SATURDAY — Strength + Volume

Pullups 5 × max reps
Dips 5 × 10
Explosive Pullups 5 × 4
Core Circuit 3 rounds

Technique Tips

Pull explosively toward the lower chest.

Lean forward during the transition.

Keep elbows close to your body.

Finish with a strong dip lockout.

Expected Progress

Athletes who follow this program consistently should develop the strength and coordination needed for their first clean muscle up.
