IRON APEX INTERMEDIATE

The Iron Apex Intermediate Protocol is designed for athletes who already have a solid foundation of calisthenics strength and are ready to begin unlocking advanced bodyweight skills.

This phase focuses on converting basic strength into real performance ability. The training structure emphasizes explosive pulling power, weighted strength development, muscle-up mechanics, and shoulder control.

The primary goal of this program is to prepare athletes for the advanced level by building the strength required for skills such as muscle-ups, handstand push-ups, and front lever progressions.

Entry Requirements

Before starting this program you should already be capable of performing the following movements with strict form.

• 10 strict pull-ups • 10-12 dips • 30 push-ups • basic core control

These benchmarks ensure that your body is prepared for the higher intensity pulling and skill work required for muscle-up training.

Training Rules

Follow these rules to get the most out of the program.

1. Every repetition must be strict. Half reps will slow your progress.

2. Rest between 2 and 3 minutes for strength exercises.

3. If you complete all sets easily, increase difficulty during the next session.

4. If you fail to complete a session, repeat the same load next week.

5. Always prioritize technique over repetition count.

Warm-Up Protocol

Before every session perform the following warm-up routine.

Dead Hang — 2 sets × 30 seconds prepare shoulders and grip
Scapular Pull Ups — 2 sets × 10 reps activate upper back muscles
Push Ups — 15 reps prepare chest and triceps
Band Pull Aparts — 20 reps activate rear delts and posture muscles
Hollow Body Hold — 30 seconds activate core compression

Muscle-Up Mechanics

The muscle-up is a complex movement that combines explosive pulling strength, swing mechanics, and a fast transition over the bar.

Most athletes fail the muscle-up because they focus only on pull-ups without learning the swing rhythm.

The correct movement sequence is:

1. hollow body position while moving forward 2. arch position while moving backward 3. explosive pull upward 4. fast transition over the bar

Swing training and explosive pulling drills in this program are specifically designed to develop this movement pattern.
