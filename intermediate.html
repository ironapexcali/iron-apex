Iron Apex Intermediate Program

This program is designed for athletes who already have basic calisthenics strength and want to begin developing advanced pulling power and skill control.

Requirements

8 pullups
10 dips
30 pushups

Goals

• Clean muscle ups
• Explosive pulling strength
• Front lever progression
• Tuck planche development
• Strong core for V-sit

Weekly Schedule

Monday — Pull Strength
Tuesday — Skill Training
Wednesday — Mobility
Thursday — Push Strength
Friday — Skill + Core
Saturday — Pull Strength
Sunday — Rest

Warmup (10 minutes)

Jump rope — 2 minutes Arm circles — 2 × 20 Scapula pullups — 3 × 10 Band shoulder mobility — 3 minutes Dead hang — 2 × 30 sec

Always warm up your shoulders before explosive pulling.

MONDAY — Pull Strength

Pullups 5 sets × 8 reps Rest: 2 minutes
Weighted Pullups 4 sets × 5 reps Rest: 2–3 minutes
High Pullups (chest to bar) 5 sets × 4 reps
Front Lever Tuck Hold 5 sets × 12 seconds
Front Lever Rows 4 sets × 6 reps
Hanging Leg Raises 4 sets × 12

Pull explosively and aim to bring the bar to your lower chest. This develops muscle-up pulling strength.

TUESDAY — Skill Training

Muscle Up Band Practice 8 attempts
Jumping Muscle Ups 6 attempts
Pullover Practice 5 attempts
Skin The Cat 4 sets × 5 reps
Handstand Practice 15 minutes
Crow Pose / Balance 5 × 30 seconds

Focus on smooth transitions over the bar when practicing muscle ups.

WEDNESDAY — Mobility Day

Deep squat hold 3 minutes
Shoulder extension stretch 2 minutes
Hamstring stretch 2 minutes
Bridge hold 3 × 20 seconds
Thoracic spine mobility 3 minutes

THURSDAY — Push Strength

Dips 5 × 10
Weighted Dips 4 × 6
Pike Pushups 5 × 8
Pseudo Planche Pushups 4 × 8
Wall Handstand Pushups 4 × 6
Plank 4 × 1 minute

Lean forward during pseudo planche pushups to load the shoulders.

FRIDAY — Skill + Core

Muscle Up Attempts 6 attempts
Swing Practice (Bar Control) 10 minutes
Tuck Front Lever Hold 5 × 12 seconds
L-Sit Hold 5 × 20 seconds
Compression Raises 4 × 12
Hanging Knee Raises 4 × 15

Compression work is key for progressing toward the V-sit.

SATURDAY — Pull Strength

Weighted Pullups 5 × 5
Explosive Pullups 5 × 4
Front Lever Tuck Rows 4 × 6
Archer Pullups 4 × 5 each side
Dead Hang 3 × 45 seconds
Dragon Flags 4 × 6

Progression Goals

Before moving to the Advanced program you should reach:

15 pullups
20 dips
Clean muscle up
10 second tuck front lever

Recovery Advice

Train hard but do not go to failure every set.

Sleep 7–9 hours per night.

Eat enough protein and calories to recover.

Consistency over months builds real calisthenics strength.
