My Story

Instead of quitting, I started rebuilding my training from the ground up. I focused on fundamentals, progressive overload, and proper skill training.

Through consistent training I eventually reached 30 pull-ups, +50kg weighted pull-ups, clean slow muscle-ups, front lever, handstand push-ups and straddle planche.

Iron Apex was created so others can reach those levels of strength without wasting years on bad training methods.

How The Iron Apex Program Works

The Iron Apex system is built around progressive overload and structured strength development. The system is divided into four levels: Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced, and Elite.

Each level follows a structured weekly routine designed to develop pulling strength, pushing power, core stability, and advanced calisthenics skills. Athletes repeat the weekly structure while gradually increasing reps, difficulty, and hold times.

Progression is based on strength standards. Athletes only move to the next level once they reach the required benchmarks. This ensures proper strength development and reduces injury risk.

Iron Apex Training Philosophy

Strength comes first. Many athletes chase advanced skills without building the strength required to control them.

Iron Apex focuses on progressive overload, strong pulling power, core control, and consistent training.
