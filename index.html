My Journey

My progress in calisthenics wasn’t fast or clean. When I started I lacked mobility, my technique was poor, and I had almost no understanding of how to structure training properly.

Like most beginners I chased skills too early. I constantly ran into plateaus because I didn't understand progression, mobility work, or how to build the foundational strength required for advanced movements.

Progress felt slow. Some skills took months of work before even small improvements appeared. I had to step back, rebuild fundamentals, and learn how the body actually adapts to strength training.

If I could restart from day one, I would train very differently. I would focus on mobility earlier, build stronger basics, and structure training to avoid the plateaus that most athletes eventually hit.

The programs on this site are built from those lessons. They focus on steady progression, strong fundamentals, and the kind of training that actually leads to advanced skills like the planche, front lever and press handstand.
