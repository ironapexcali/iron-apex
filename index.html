My Story

When I first started calisthenics I couldn’t do a single pull-up. I lacked mobility, strength and constantly hit frustrating plateaus.

Instead of quitting, I started focusing on progressive overload, fundamental strength and skill-specific training.

Over time that approach allowed me to reach:

• 30 strict pull-ups • +50kg weighted pull-ups • clean slow muscle-ups • front lever progressions • handstand push-ups • straddle planche strength

Iron Apex was created to help athletes follow a structured path toward these skills without wasting years on ineffective training.

Training Programs

Muscle-Up Program

Requirements: 10 pull-ups / 12 dips
4 training sessions per week focused on explosive pulling power, high pull-ups and muscle-up technique.

Beginner

0–5 pull-ups / 0–5 dips
3–4 sessions per week focused on pull-up strength, push-up strength and core stability.

Intermediate

8 pull-ups / 10 dips
5 weekly sessions building explosive pull-ups, muscle-up strength and handstand control.

Advanced

15 pull-ups / freestanding handstand
Front lever progressions, weighted calisthenics and advanced strength development.

Elite

Muscle-up / front lever
Elite-level calisthenics strength including planche, advanced pulling power and high-level body control.

How The Iron Apex System Works

The Iron Apex system is built around progressive overload and structured strength development.

Athletes first build foundational strength through pull-ups, push-ups and dips before progressing into explosive pulling, advanced bodyweight skills and weighted calisthenics.

Each level builds on the previous one so strength develops step-by-step rather than randomly.
