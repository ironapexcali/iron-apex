Program Philosophy

Most beginner athletes train randomly. They perform workouts that make them tired but never actually increase their strength.

Iron Apex is different. Every exercise in this program is chosen for a specific purpose. Each training session builds strength that directly translates into better calisthenics performance.

Instead of focusing on random high-repetition workouts, this system focuses on building the three most important strength qualities:

• pulling strength • pushing strength • core control

These three areas form the foundation of every advanced calisthenics skill.

Starting Requirements

Before beginning this program you should already be able to perform:

• 3 strict pull-ups • 5 dips • 10 push-ups

If you cannot perform these movements yet you should complete the Baseline Program first.

Training Rules

Strict Form Only

Every repetition should be performed with control and full range of motion. Kipping, swinging and partial reps reduce strength development.

Consistency

Strength is built through consistent training over time. Missing workouts will slow your progress.

Progress Gradually

Each week your goal should be to improve slightly. This could mean more repetitions, better form, or longer hold times.

Weekly Training Structure

Training is performed five days per week with two rest days. Each session targets specific movement patterns to develop balanced strength.

Monday — Pull Strength

Pull-ups — 5 sets max reps focus on controlled repetitions and chest to bar
Australian Rows — 4 x 12 reps build upper back and pulling endurance
Dead Hangs — 3 sets max time develop grip and shoulder stability
Hanging Knee Raises — 4 x 10 reps build core control for advanced skills

Tuesday — Push Strength

Push-ups — 5 sets max reps maintain strict body alignment
Dips — 4 x 8 reps lower slowly and control the bottom position
Scapular Push-ups — 3 x 12 reps develop shoulder stability
Plank Hold — 3 x 60 seconds improve core endurance

Wednesday — Active Recovery

20–30 minute walk
Mobility exercises for shoulders and wrists

Thursday — Pull Volume

Pull-ups — 6 sets of 4–6 reps
Inverted Rows — 4 x 12 reps
Scapular Pull-ups — 4 x 10 reps
Hanging Leg Raises — 3 x 10 reps

Friday — Push + Core

Push-ups — 4 sets max reps
Dips — 4 x 10 reps
L-Sit Holds — 4 x 10 seconds
Hollow Body Hold — 3 x 30 seconds

Saturday — Full Body

Pull-ups — 4 x 6 reps
Push-ups — 4 x 15 reps
Dips — 3 x 12 reps
Core Circuit — 3 rounds

Sunday — Rest

Full recovery day
