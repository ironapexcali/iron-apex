Beginner Calisthenics Program

This program builds the foundation for calisthenics strength. Your goal is to develop pulling strength, pushing strength, core control and balance before moving into harder skills.

Requirements

0–5 pullups
0–5 dips
0–20 pushups

Session Structure (~1h30)

Every training day follows this structure:

  • 10–15 min warm-up
  • 20 min skill practice
  • 40 min strength training
  • 10–15 min core work
  • 10 min mobility

Warm-Up Routine

  • Jump rope – 3 minutes
  • Shoulder circles – 30 reps
  • Wrist warm-up – 2 minutes
  • Scapula pullups – 2x10
  • Light pushups – 2x15

Weekly Schedule

Monday — Pull Strength

  • Pullups – 5 sets max
  • Australian rows – 4x12
  • Negative pullups – 4x6
  • Dead hang – 3x30s
  • Hanging knee raises – 4x12
  • Plank – 3x60s

Tuesday — Push Strength

  • Pushups – 5x20
  • Dips – 4x6
  • Pike pushups – 4x10
  • Incline pushups – 3x15
  • L-sit tuck holds – 6 attempts

Wednesday — Rest

Thursday — Balance Skills

  • Frog stand practice – 10 min
  • Crow pose holds – 10 min
  • Elbow lever practice – 10 min
  • Wall handstand practice – 15 min
  • L-sit practice

Friday — Strength Day

  • Pullups – 5x5
  • Pushups – 4x25
  • Dips – 4x8
  • Hanging knee raises – 4x12
  • Plank – 3x60s

Saturday — Mobility Day

  • Shoulder mobility routine
  • Pancake stretch
  • Bridge progression
  • Hamstring stretch
  • Wrist conditioning

Sunday — Rest

Progression Tips

Focus on clean technique. Quality reps matter more than doing more repetitions.

When you can perform 8–10 clean pullups and 10+ dips, you are ready to move into the Intermediate program.
